Advancing Prognostic and Health Management through Large Language Models: Intelligent predictive maintenance, fault diagnosis, and remaining useful life prediction for industrial systems.

Abstract

We present PHM-LLM, a novel approach that integrates Large Language Models (LLMs) with Prognostic and Health Management (PHM) systems to revolutionize predictive maintenance and fault diagnosis. Our framework leverages the reasoning capabilities of LLMs to interpret complex sensor data, predict equipment failures, and provide actionable maintenance recommendations. Through comprehensive evaluation on standard PHM benchmarks including NASA C-MAPSS and PHM08 Challenge datasets, we demonstrate significant improvements in remaining useful life (RUL) prediction accuracy and fault classification performance. The system integrates multi-modal sensor data processing with natural language understanding, enabling intuitive human-AI interaction for maintenance decision-making.

Problem Statement

Traditional PHM approaches face significant challenges in handling complex, multi-modal industrial data and providing interpretable maintenance decisions. Key challenges include:

  • Data Complexity: Industrial systems generate diverse sensor data requiring sophisticated analysis
  • Interpretability: Black-box models lack explainability for critical maintenance decisions
  • Domain Knowledge Integration: Difficulty incorporating expert knowledge and maintenance procedures
  • Real-time Decision Making: Need for rapid, actionable insights for operational systems
